Bedlam is a Hysteria 2 client built directly on the protocol core. It runs the upstream Hysteria 2 engine, the same Go implementation as the official client, and drives it from Kotlin. There is no bundled binary and no local SOCKS5 or HTTP proxy in between. Your traffic enters a TUN interface, passes through an in-process userspace network stack, and leaves over QUIC. The whole device is tunnelled, so individual apps have nothing to point at.

What it does:

- The full Hysteria 2 configuration: authentication, TLS (custom SNI, custom CA, certificate pinning, mutual TLS), Salamander obfuscation, QUIC tuning, BBR and Brutal congestion control, bandwidth limits, and port hopping.
- A real TUN device backed by a gVisor userspace stack, carrying TCP and UDP, with DNS resolved through the tunnel and cached.
- One-tap import of hysteria2:// and hy2:// links.
- Per-app allow and block lists, and rule-based split tunnelling by CIDR, autonomous system, or domain.
- Reconnects on its own through QUIC keep-alive and recovers across Wi-Fi and mobile changes.
- A Quick Settings tile, always-on VPN, and a foreground service tuned for aggressive battery management.

No analytics, no tracking. Open source under GPL-3.0.
