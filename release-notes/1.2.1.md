VPN reliability fixes for aggressive phone firmware.

- Bedlam now remembers when the VPN was supposed to be running. If Android or the phone firmware kills the app process, reopening Bedlam can recover the active profile instead of showing an idle dashboard.
- The dashboard and Quick Settings tile now use the persisted VPN runtime state while the tunnel is recovering, so the app is less likely to show "Disconnected" while Android is still restarting the service.
- Normal disconnects clear that runtime state, so Bedlam does not accidentally reconnect after you intentionally stop the tunnel.
- The Keep VPN alive checklist now calls out the extra OnePlus, OPPO, and realme settings that matter on current firmware builds, including background activity, foreground activity, autostart, Sleep standby or Deep optimization, and locking Bedlam in Recents.
- Startup logs now include Android's last recorded app exit reason, which should make future vendor kill reports easier to diagnose.
